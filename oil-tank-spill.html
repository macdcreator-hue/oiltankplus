Oil Tank Leak or Spill? Here's What to Do Immediately

By Oil Tanks Plus 4 min read July 2024

An oil spill is stressful, but the actions you take in the first hour make an enormous difference to the environmental impact and your legal exposure. Stay calm and follow these steps.

Emergency Contacts

If oil has entered a watercourse (stream, river, drain, ditch), call the Environment Agency 24-hour hotline: 0800 80 70 60 immediately — you are legally required to report this. For Scotland: SEPA on 0800 80 70 60. For Wales: Natural Resources Wales on 0300 065 3000.

Step 1: Stop the Source of the Spill

If the leak is coming from a pipe fitting or valve, turn off the isolation valve closest to the tank. This is usually located on the outlet pipe at the base of the tank. If you can't isolate the leak, try to slow it by placing a container under the drip.

Do NOT attempt to repair the tank yourself with sealant or tape — this is a temporary fix at best and may not stop the flow. Call an OFTEC engineer.

Step 2: Prevent the Oil Spreading

Your priority is to stop oil reaching:

  • Drains and gullies — cover or block these with sand, earth or drain mats if available
  • Any watercourse — even a garden ditch counts
  • Neighbouring properties

Use dry sand, cat litter, sawdust or absorbent granules to create a berm around the spill and soak up the oil. Do not use water — it will spread the oil further.

Step 3: Report the Spill

If oil has entered a drain, gully or watercourse, you must report it to the Environment Agency (or equivalent devolved body) immediately. Failure to report is a criminal offence.

Call 0800 80 70 60 (England and Wales, 24 hours) and provide your name, address, and a description of what has happened.

Your home insurer should also be notified as soon as practicable — do this on the same day if possible. Oil spill remediation can be expensive, and your insurer needs to be involved early.

Step 4: Do Not Clean Up Yourself

It's tempting to try to mop up the oil, but there are legal implications to how contaminated material is disposed of. Oil-soaked soil, sand and absorbent material is classified as controlled waste and must be disposed of through a licensed waste carrier — it cannot go in your bin.

A licensed environmental remediation company can assess the extent of contamination, collect the waste and provide documentation. Your insurer will usually appoint one.

Step 5: Secure the Damaged Tank

Until the tank can be professionally assessed or replaced, keep children and animals away from the area. If the tank is significantly damaged and still contains fuel, call an OFTEC engineer to arrange emergency pump-out.

Step 6: Replace the Tank

Following a significant spill, tank replacement is almost always the right outcome. A bunded replacement tank means that if a future inner tank failure occurs, the oil is contained within the outer bund — preventing a repeat of this situation.

Quick Summary — What to Do

  1. Isolate the leak at the valve
  2. Stop oil reaching drains and watercourses with sand or absorbent material
  3. If oil entered a watercourse: call 0800 80 70 60 immediately
  4. Notify your home insurer
  5. Do not clean up yourself — wait for professional remediation
  6. Arrange emergency pump-out and tank replacement
