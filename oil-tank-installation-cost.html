How Much Does Oil Tank Installation Cost in the UK?

By Oil Tanks Plus 5 min read January 2025

Oil tank installation costs vary depending on tank type, size, access, base preparation and what's being done with your old tank. Here's a transparent breakdown of what you can expect to pay — and how to avoid being overcharged.

Typical Total Costs for a Domestic Installation

For a standard domestic replacement (existing tank removed, new bunded tank installed on an existing prepared base), typical all-in costs range from £1,200 to £2,500. This includes the tank, installation labour, old tank removal and the OFTEC certificate.

A brand-new installation where no tank previously existed — with base preparation included — typically runs from £1,500 to £3,500 depending on access and complexity.

Cost Breakdown by Component

1. The Tank Itself

The tank is usually the largest single cost:

  • Single-skin plastic (1,000–2,500 litre): £300–£700
  • Bunded plastic (1,000–2,500 litre): £600–£1,400
  • Steel bunded tank: £1,000–£2,500+
  • Fire-rated tank: £1,200–£3,000+

Larger tanks cost proportionally more. Industrial and commercial tanks are quoted individually.

2. Installation Labour

Fitting a standard domestic tank typically costs £250–£600 in labour, depending on your location, access and how much groundwork is involved. This covers positioning the tank, connecting pipework, fitting a fire valve, commissioning and certification.

3. Old Tank Removal

When you're replacing an old tank, removal and disposal is typically included free of charge with Oil Tanks Plus installations. We pump out residual oil, decontaminate the tank and take it away for responsible recycling. If you need standalone removal (no new tank), see our tank disposal service.

4. Base Preparation

If there's no existing base, you'll need one built. A standard concrete plinth costs £200–£500 for materials and labour. Purpose-made plastic bunded bases are an alternative and can be quicker to install.

5. Pipework

If your oil line needs extending, re-routing or upgrading, expect to add £100–£400 for pipework. Underground pipes may need protective ducting, which adds cost but is a one-time investment that protects against corrosion.

What Affects the Final Price?

  • Tank size: larger tanks cost more to supply and may need crane offload
  • Access: restricted access (narrow gates, steps, distance from road) adds time and cost
  • Location: rural properties with long travel distances may incur a call-out supplement
  • Condition of existing pipework: if your oil line is old or leaking, replacement adds cost
  • Groundwork: if the ground needs excavating or a new concrete base building, allow extra

Is Oil Tank Installation Covered by Insurance?

Some home insurance policies include oil tank replacement as part of their heating system cover, particularly if the failure is sudden and accidental. It's worth checking your policy before requesting quotes. We can also provide documentation to support an insurance claim if required.

How to Get the Best Value

  • Get at least three quotes from OFTEC-registered installers
  • Don't choose purely on price — check that an OFTEC certificate is included
  • Confirm whether old tank removal is included in the quoted price
  • Ask about warranty on both the tank and the installation workmanship
  • Check that the engineer is currently OFTEC-registered (not just previously)
