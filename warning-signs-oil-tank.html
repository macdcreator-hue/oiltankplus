5 Warning Signs Your Oil Tank Needs Replacing

By Oil Tanks Plus 4 min read February 2025

An ageing or deteriorating oil tank is one of those problems that announces itself quietly — until it doesn't. A small hairline crack, a corroding fitting, or a slow seep can become a catastrophic spill with no warning. Knowing what to look for can save you thousands and protect the environment.

Warning Sign 1: Visible Cracks, Crazing or Bulging

Polyethylene tanks are designed to last 10–20 years under normal conditions, but UV exposure, ground movement and temperature cycling all take a toll. Run your eye along the tank walls, especially at the base, corners and around any fittings. Look for:

  • Fine surface crazing (a spider-web pattern of tiny cracks)
  • Deeper cracks or splits, particularly near fitting points
  • Any bulging or deformation in the tank walls
  • White stress marks, especially under fittings and valves

Surface crazing alone doesn't always mean imminent failure, but it's a sign the tank is ageing and should be assessed by a professional. Deep cracks or structural deformation mean the tank should be replaced promptly.

Warning Sign 2: Rust, Corrosion or Pitting on Steel Tanks

Steel tanks are susceptible to external corrosion, particularly in humid or coastal environments. Look for:

  • Surface rust — orange-brown staining on the outside of the tank
  • Pitting — small craters in the steel surface indicating deeper corrosion
  • Rust streaks running down from the tank base or fittings
  • Soft spots — areas where the steel has thinned below structural integrity

Surface rust on a steel tank doesn't always require immediate replacement, but deep pitting or thinning of the steel wall does. An OFTEC engineer can advise on whether remediation or replacement is the right call.

Warning Sign 3: A Persistent Smell of Oil or Fuel

If you can smell heating oil around or near your tank — not just after a delivery — there is almost certainly a slow leak somewhere. The source could be:

  • A failing tank fitting (most leaks start at fittings, not through the tank wall)
  • A corroded or cracked oil line between the tank and boiler
  • The fire valve or filter assembly
  • A pinhole crack in the tank wall itself

Don't ignore an oil smell. Even a slow drip can contaminate soil and groundwater over time, and you — as the property owner — bear legal responsibility for remediation costs.

Warning Sign 4: Unexplained Heating Failures

If your boiler is regularly losing prime, running out of oil faster than expected, or cutting out for no obvious reason, the problem could be in your tank rather than your boiler. Common tank-related causes of heating failure include:

  • Sludge buildup on the tank floor contaminating the oil supply
  • Water ingress (condensation or a cracked fitting letting rainwater in)
  • A failing or stuck fire valve
  • Debris from a corroding internal wall entering the oil line

A fuel polishing service can sometimes resolve sludge and contamination issues. If the tank is found to have internal corrosion or structural issues, replacement is the safer route.

Warning Sign 5: Your Tank Is More Than 10–15 Years Old

There's no fixed lifespan for an oil tank — some plastic tanks last 20+ years, some fail at 12. But if your tank is over 15 years old and you don't know its history, it's worth having it formally inspected. The risk of failure increases significantly with age, and an unexpected leak is far more disruptive — and expensive — than a planned replacement.

If you don't know the age of your tank, check for a date stamp moulded into the plastic (usually near the base or under a fitting). For steel tanks, check any documentation from when the property was purchased.
